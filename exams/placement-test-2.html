Passage 1: Tidal Energy

Tidal energy is a form of renewable power generated by the rise and fall of ocean tides. Unlike solar or wind power, tides are highly predictable, which makes electricity output easy to forecast. Tidal barrages, built across estuaries, capture water at high tide and release it through turbines as the tide falls. One major advantage is that tidal plants produce no greenhouse gases during operation. However, the initial construction costs are extremely high, and barrages can disturb local marine ecosystems by altering the natural flow of water. For these reasons, tidal energy currently supplies only a tiny fraction of the world's electricity, although interest in the technology continues to grow.

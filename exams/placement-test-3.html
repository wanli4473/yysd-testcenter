Passage 1: Urban Green Spaces

Urban green spaces, such as parks and community gardens, provide significant benefits to city dwellers. Research suggests that regular access to greenery can lower stress levels and encourage physical activity. Trees also improve air quality by absorbing pollutants and releasing oxygen, while their shade helps to cool streets during heatwaves. In addition, green spaces can support local wildlife, offering habitats for birds and insects. However, maintaining these areas requires ongoing funding, and in densely built cities suitable land is often scarce and expensive. Despite these challenges, many urban planners now regard green spaces as an essential part of healthy, sustainable city design.
